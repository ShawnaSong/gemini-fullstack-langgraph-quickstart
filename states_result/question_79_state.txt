**1. What is the Alien Enemies Act?**

*   The Alien Enemies Act of 1798 is a wartime law that allows the president to detain or deport citizens or "natives" (individuals born in an enemy state) of a hostile nation during a declared war or in the event of an "invasion or predatory incursion" against the U.S. [2, 3, 17]. [time](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/id/2-0) [cbsnews](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/id/2-1)

*   It was part of the Alien and Sedition Acts, passed when the U.S. anticipated war with France [5, 11, 16]. [cbsnews](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/id/2-1)
*   The Act has been invoked only a few times: during the War of 1812, World War I, and World War II [2, 5, 15]. [politico](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/id/2-3)
